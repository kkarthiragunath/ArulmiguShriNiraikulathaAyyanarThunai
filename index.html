Our Lineage and Historical Anchor

To know your Kuladeivam is to know your cosmic coordinates. For centuries, our families have journeyed back to the soil of the temple to present newborns, seek blessings for weddings, and express absolute gratitude during festival cycles.

Located At

Shri Niraikulatha iyyanar temple is located north of Vadakkuputhur Village, Sankarankovil Taluk, Tirunelveli District. The Kuladeivam of many villages.

Interesting Thing

Interesting thing about the temple is, a copy of this temple had been created in Vettarampatty Village, located 15 Kilometers in the south-west direction from this temple.

Pidiman Concept

Building of new temple is done by applying the principle of pidiman (gathering soil sample from the original temple and mixing the soil sample in the foundation of the new temple).

Village Ecosystem Links

Worshipped by 3 villages

Original temple located at Naluvasankottai, is being worshipped by more than 100 families of 3 villages - (Vadakkuputhur, Veeriruppu and Pattakurichi). Often referred as - Tamil Yadavas (Konars/Karayalars).

Worshipped by 7 villages

New temple located at Vettarampatty, is being worshipped by more than 350 families of 7 Villages - (Vettarampatty, Periyasamypuram, Sanganapery, Krishnapuram, Shenbaganallur, Achchampatti, Muthusamyapuram).

400 years Migration

Its believed that, Worshippers of new temple have migrated from Vadakkuputhur area to neighbouring areas in the west direction 400 years back. However they created the new temple just 100 years back. Its clear that some common link existed between these two group of people.
